# The Professional

I run plants. That sentence has carried me through twenty-five years of food and manufacturing, and I have come to distrust every shorter version of it. To run a plant is to be responsible for a place where raw material, machinery, money, and people meet under time pressure, and where the gap between how things are supposed to work and how they actually work is paid for in cash and in someone's afternoon. The job is to close that gap without breaking anyone in the process.

I came up through operations, but I spent part of my earlier career on the strategy and acquisition side, where businesses are studied from a distance and reduced to their logic. I don't regret that schooling — it taught me the questions a board is actually asking — but it also taught me how much the model leaves out. The most useful thing I can do for a company is hold both pictures at once: the floor and the spreadsheet, the smell of the room and the shape of the margin, and refuse to pretend either one tells the whole truth.

## A word about why I'm looking

I'm drawn to companies that treat operations as something more than a cost to be managed — places where the way the work is done is understood to be part of how the business wins. I'd rather wait for that than talk my way into its opposite.
