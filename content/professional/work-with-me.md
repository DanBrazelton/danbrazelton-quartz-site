# On working together

I'll keep this honest rather than promotional, because a page that tries too hard to sell you something is usually hiding how little it has to offer.

Sometimes a company needs senior operating judgment without adding a permanent seat — a plant to be steadied through a transition, a system to be put in properly, real cost to be found and taken out in a way that holds. That is work I'm willing to step into, on a fractional or interim basis, when the problem is genuinely interesting and the people are serious about fixing it rather than being seen to.

The problems I'm drawn to are the ones I've spent a career on: an operation that needs to get leaner without losing what made it work; an ERP that needs to go in, or be rescued, with the human change managed as carefully as the configuration; a food-safety program that has to become real instead of ceremonial. I work mostly with food, beverage, and consumer-goods makers, and I have a particular fondness for the Pacific Northwest, where a lot of good things are made by people who care about making them well.

I'm not the right answer for every situation, and I'd rather tell you that early than discover it late. If what you're facing sounds like the above, the way to start is a conversation — no pitch, just an honest look at whether I can actually help.
