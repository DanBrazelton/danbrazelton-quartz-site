# How I think about the work

What follows is not a methodology with a trademark. It is a set of convictions I've earned the slow way, and the seed of most of what I'll eventually write in [Field Notes](/field-notes/).

**Go and look.** The single most reliable error in management is believing the report instead of the room. A number is a rumor until you have stood where it was made. Before I change anything, I spend time on the floor and with the people who run it, because the real problem is almost never the one being complained about in the meeting.

**Measure before you cut.** Cost comes out cleanly only when you understand what each dollar was buying. Cut first and measure later and you will break something you didn't know was load-bearing, then spend twice putting it back. The discipline is to make the work visible first, so that what you remove was genuinely waste and not capability in disguise.

**Hold the opposing idea.** The best operating decisions live in the tension between two things that both seem true — speed and quality, cost and capability, the model and the floor. The lazy move is to pick a side and call it focus. The better move is to sit in the discomfort long enough to design the option that honors both. Most false economies are just someone who refused to.

**Build the system, then the team, then leave.** Durable results come from process, not from heroics that depend on a few exhausted people. I build the standard work and the system of record first, so the team has something solid to own. Then I develop the team to run and improve it. Then the measure of whether I did it well is whether it keeps running after I'm gone.

**The people are not a resource.** They are the operation. I have led union shops and learned that respect is not a soft skill — it is the precondition for every hard change you will ever ask them to survive. Treat that as overhead and you will pay for it, on a schedule you don't control.

These are short on purpose. Each one is an essay waiting to be written, and over time they will be — over in [Field Notes](/field-notes/).
