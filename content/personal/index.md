# The Personal

The attention I bring to a plant does not switch off when I leave it. It just stops being paid, and gets pointed at things I do purely because I want to. Two of them have lasted long enough to be worth a page: cooking, and amateur radio. People file those under recreation, as if they were the opposite of work. They are not the opposite of work. They are work I get to do for love, which is a different and better thing.

What connects them — and connects them back to everything on [the professional side](/professional/) — is that both are systems you can fall in love with. A kitchen rewards the same attention a production line does: respect the process, taste as you go, keep honest notes. A radio is physics you can hold in your hands, a quiet argument with the atmosphere that you win or lose on your own preparation. Neither lets you fake it. That's most of why I love them.
