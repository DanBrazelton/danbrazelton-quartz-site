# 🌿 Ottolenghi's Chargrilled Broccoli Rabe


> **Quick Summary:** Ottolenghi's signature brassica treatment, adapted for broccoli rabe. A quick blanch sets the colour and pulls out the harsh bitterness; a ripping-hot grill pan adds smoky char; and a warm garlic-and-chilli oil poured over at the end ties it together. Bright, bitter, smoky, and a little spicy.
> **Source:** Adapted from Yotam Ottolenghi — *Ottolenghi: The Cookbook*

## 🛒 Ingredients
* 1 bunch **Broccoli rabe (rapini)**
* 60g (4 tbsp) **Olive oil**, divided — 15g for charring, 45g for the chilli oil
* 3 cloves **Garlic**, thinly sliced
* 1 **Red chilli** (mild), thinly sliced
* ½ **Lemon**
* 20g **Flaked almonds**, toasted
* **Flaky sea salt** & **black pepper** (to taste)

## 🧑‍🍳 Instructions

```cooklang
>> servings: 1

Bring a #large pot of well-salted water to a rolling boil and set a #bowl of ice water alongside.

Trim the dry ends from the @broccoli rabe{1%bunch} and blanch in the boiling water for ~{2%minutes}, just until bright green. Immediately lift into the ice bath with a #slotted spoon to stop the cooking and lock in the colour. (The blanch also tames the natural bitterness of rabe.)

Drain well and pat completely dry with a #clean towel — any clinging water will steam rather than char.

Toss the dried rabe with @olive oil{15%g} and a pinch of @salt{}. Place a #ridged grill pan (or cast-iron skillet) over high heat for at least ~{5%minutes}, until ripping hot.

Char the rabe in a single, uncrowded layer, turning, until char marks appear all over — about ~{4%minutes} total. Work in batches if needed and pile the charred rabe onto a #serving platter.

Meanwhile, gently warm @olive oil{45%g} in a #small saucepan with the sliced @garlic{3%cloves} and @red chilli{1%thinly sliced} over medium heat until the garlic just turns pale gold, about ~{3%minutes}. Do not let it brown or it will go bitter.

Pour the warm garlic-chilli oil over the charred rabe and toss well. Finish with a squeeze of @lemon{0.5}, @flaky sea salt{to taste}, @black pepper{to taste}, and a scatter of @flaked almonds{20%g}.

Serve warm or at room temperature.
```

## 💡 Notes & Tweaks
* **Why blanch rabe:** Unlike the asparagus method, rabe genuinely benefits from the 2-minute boil — bitterness is water-soluble and leaches out, while the ice bath keeps it vivid green. Don't skip the ice bath or it'll overcook into khaki.
* **Get the pan screaming hot:** The char only happens on a dry surface at high heat. Wet rabe + a lukewarm pan = grey and steamed. Dry thoroughly and preheat fully.
* **Don't brown the garlic:** Pull the chilli oil off the heat at pale gold. Browned garlic turns the whole dish acrid.
* **Variations:** Ottolenghi often finishes with toasted almonds and lemon; a shaving of Parmesan or pecorino, or a spoon of tahini-yogurt underneath, both work beautifully against the bitterness.
